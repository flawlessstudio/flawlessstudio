# Archetype 13 — Design / Brand / Creative

> **Flawless Method** | `docs/archetypes/` | v1.0.0 | 2026-05-25

---

## Identity

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Output type** | Brand identity, design system, creative campaign, or visual communication asset |
| **Novelty class** | Mark 1 — creative direction undefined at start; visual language emergent |
| **Primary standard** | Brand identity methodology (Interbrand, Wolff Olins), WCAG 2.2, ISO 9241 |
| **Sector** | Any — Product, Corporate, Retail, Digital, Cultural, Public Sector |

---

## Scope

All activities from brand strategy and creative brief through concept development, design execution, system documentation, and asset delivery. Covers brand identity, design systems, UI/UX design, and creative campaign production.

---

## Exclusions

| Out of scope | Redirect to |
|---|---|
| Software product with design as a component | Archetype 01 |
| Marketing campaign strategy and execution | Archetype 14 |
| Service design including operational model | Archetype 12 |
| Hardware product industrial design | Archetype 05 |

---

## Primary Use Cases

- Brand identity design or rebrand
- Design system creation (tokens, components, guidelines)
- Visual identity for a product launch
- Creative campaign concept and production
- UI/UX design for a digital product

---

## Phase Structure

| # | Phase | Key outputs |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Brand strategy** | Brand positioning, personality, values, competitive audit, creative brief |
| 2 | **Concept development** | Mood boards, creative territories, initial concepts (min 3) |
| 3 | **Design development** | Refined concept, logo/wordmark, colour, typography, grid, motion |
| 4 | **System documentation** | Brand guidelines, design tokens, component library, usage rules |
| 5 | **Asset production** | All required formats and sizes, across all channels and media |
| 6 | **Rollout & governance** | Asset library published, brand governance process active |

---

## Quality Controls

- Creative brief signed off before any concept development begins
- Minimum 3 distinct creative concepts presented at concept stage
- Accessibility audit on final identity (contrast ratios, legibility at minimum sizes)
- Design tokens documented and version-controlled
- Asset checklist complete before delivery (all formats, sizes, dark/light variants)
- Brand guidelines published in accessible format (PDF + web)
- Usage rules cover both correct and incorrect applications

---

## Validation Gates

| Gate | Criteria | Who approves |
|---|---|---|
| **G1 — Creative brief** | Brief signed off, budget and timeline agreed | Creative Lead + Client/Sponsor |
| **G2 — Concept selection** | One concept selected from minimum 3 presented | Creative Director + Sponsor |
| **G3 — Design development** | Visual identity approved in all key applications | Creative Director + Sponsor |
| **G4 — System complete** | Guidelines published, tokens documented, components built | Creative Lead + Design Lead |
| **G5 — Asset delivery** | All assets delivered, checklist verified, formats confirmed | Creative Lead + Client |

---

## Resilience & Recovery

- Version control on all design source files from day 1
- IP ownership clauses in all creative contracts before work begins
- Font and asset licensing verified before production
- Rollback option to previous version if rebrand is reversed
- Brand governance owner named before guidelines go live

---

## International Validity

| Standard | Scope |
|---|---|
| WCAG 2.2 AA | Accessibility for digital applications of identity |
| ISO 9241-210:2019 | Human-centred design |
| W3C Design Tokens Community Group | Design token interoperability |
| Figma / Storybook ecosystem | Component and token delivery standard |
| ADA / EAA | Accessibility compliance (US / EU) |

---

## Lock Condition

**Locks when:** Brand guidelines published, asset library live, governance owner named, and all contracted assets delivered and accepted.

**Reopens when:** Rebrand triggered by acquisition, market repositioning, product pivot, or scheduled brand refresh cycle.
