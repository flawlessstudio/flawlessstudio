# Archetype 07 — Research & Development

> **Flawless Method** | `docs/archetypes/` | v1.0.0 | 2026-05-24

---

## Identity

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Output type** | New knowledge, validated hypothesis, functional prototype, or patentable result |
| **Novelty class** | Mark 1 — output and solution both undefined at start; path built iteratively |
| **Primary standard** | ISO 56002:2019 (innovation management), Frascati Manual 2015, TRL methodology |
| **Sector** | Science, Pharma, Deep Tech, Academia, Advanced Engineering, Defence R&D |

---

## Scope

All activities from research question definition through literature review, experimental design, execution, analysis, validation, knowledge transfer, and IP protection. Covers basic research, applied research, and experimental development.

---

## Exclusions

| Out of scope | Redirect to |
|---|---|
| Product with a defined target ready for execution | Archetype 05 |
| AI system with a defined deployment target | Archetype 02 |
| Manufacturing optimisation of a known process | Archetype 06 |
| Clinical trial execution (regulatory path dominant) | Archetype 11 |

---

## Primary Use Cases

- Applied research programme in science or engineering
- Drug discovery and early-stage pharmaceutical development
- Novel materials, chemical, or biological process development
- Deep tech prototype (quantum, photonics, biotech)
- Patent filing and IP development programme

---

## Phase Structure

| # | Phase | Key outputs |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Hypothesis definition** | Research question, success criteria, TRL baseline, ethics review |
| 2 | **Literature review** | State of the art, gap analysis, prior art search (IP) |
| 3 | **Experimental design** | Methodology, controls, resources, timeline, risk plan |
| 4 | **Execution** | Experiments, data collection, lab notebooks, version control |
| 5 | **Analysis** | Statistical analysis, results interpretation, peer review |
| 6 | **Validation** | Independent replication, external review, TRL assessment |
| 7 | **Transfer & protection** | Publication, patent filing, technology transfer, lessons learned |

---

## Quality Controls

- Research question is falsifiable and measurable
- Methodology peer-reviewed before execution
- Reproducibility controls documented (protocol, materials, equipment calibration)
- Ethics approval obtained where required (human subjects, animals, biosafety)
- Data versioning and lab notebook integrity (signed and dated)
- IP audit before publication or external disclosure
- TRL gate documented at end of each phase

---

## Validation Gates

| Gate | Criteria | Who approves |
|---|---|---|
| **G1 — Research design** | Hypothesis approved, methodology validated, ethics cleared | PI + Research Director |
| **G2 — Mid-point review** | Data quality confirmed, hypothesis still viable or pivot decided | PI + Sponsor |
| **G3 — Results** | Statistical significance met, reproducibility confirmed | PI + Peer Reviewer |
| **G4 — IP & transfer** | Patent/publication cleared, IP ownership documented | PI + Legal / TTO |

---

## Resilience & Recovery

- Pivot protocol defined: if hypothesis fails at G2, structured decision to pivot or kill
- Data backup and versioning from day 1
- Regulatory compliance plan for studies involving humans, animals, or controlled substances
- Knowledge transfer plan ensures outputs survive personnel changes
- Contingency resource plan for long-lead reagents or equipment

---

## International Validity

| Standard | Scope |
|---|---|
| ISO 56002:2019 | Innovation management system |
| Frascati Manual 2015 | R&D classification (OECD) |
| GLP (OECD / 21 CFR Part 58) | Good laboratory practice |
| ICH guidelines | Pharma R&D (international) |
| Helsinki Declaration | Ethics in human research |
| TRL 1–9 | Technology readiness levels (NASA / EU Horizon) |
| GDPR / HIPAA | Data protection in research |

---

## Lock Condition

**Locks when:** Output is archived, IP status resolved (filed, published, or waived), and knowledge transfer or publication complete.

**Reopens when:** New funding cycle, follow-on research phase, IP challenge, or unexpected experimental result triggers re-investigation.
