# Archetype 12 — Service Design / Delivery

> **Flawless Method** | `docs/archetypes/` | v1.0.0 | 2026-05-25

---

## Identity

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Output type** | Designed, piloted, and scaled service — physical, digital, or hybrid |
| **Novelty class** | Mark 1–n — user need known; service form, touchpoints, and delivery model variable |
| **Primary standard** | Service Design (IDEO, Double Diamond), ITIL 4, ISO 9241, NN/g UX standards |
| **Sector** | Healthcare, Retail, Finance, Government, Hospitality, Transport, Education, Digital Services |

---

## Scope

All activities from service discovery through concept design, prototyping, piloting, scaling, and continuous improvement. Covers front-stage (customer-facing) and back-stage (operational) service design, including digital and physical touchpoints.

---

## Exclusions

| Out of scope | Redirect to |
|---|---|
| Software product development only | Archetype 01 |
| Organisational restructuring as main output | Archetype 08 |
| Marketing campaign without service redesign | Archetype 14 |
| Regulatory compliance programme | Archetype 11 |

---

## Primary Use Cases

- New service concept design and launch
- Customer experience (CX) transformation
- Healthcare patient journey redesign
- Government digital service design (GDS standard)
- Omnichannel service integration

---

## Phase Structure

| # | Phase | Key outputs |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Discovery** | User research, service safari, jobs-to-be-done map, insight synthesis |
| 2 | **Define** | Service opportunity, user personas, current-state service blueprint |
| 3 | **Ideation** | Concept generation, service concept sketches, value proposition canvas |
| 4 | **Prototyping** | Lo-fi and hi-fi service prototypes, wizard-of-oz tests, storyboards |
| 5 | **Pilot** | Live pilot with real users, KPI tracking, operations dry run |
| 6 | **Scale** | Rollout plan, staff training, operational handover, feedback loop active |
| 7 | **Improvement** | NPS / CSAT tracking, service review cycle, iteration backlog |

---

## Quality Controls

- Minimum 10 user research interviews before Define phase
- Service blueprint covers all frontstage and backstage touchpoints
- Prototype tested with minimum 5 representative users before pilot
- Pilot KPIs defined and baselined before go-live
- Staff training completion ≥ 90% before scale
- NPS / CSAT baseline established in pilot; improvement target set for scale
- Accessibility audit completed before public launch (WCAG 2.2 AA for digital)

---

## Validation Gates

| Gate | Criteria | Who approves |
|---|---|---|
| **G1 — Insight** | Research synthesis validated, opportunity framed | Service Lead + Sponsor |
| **G2 — Concept** | Service concept selected, value proposition tested | Service Lead + Business Owner |
| **G3 — Prototype** | Usability test passed, no critical UX failures | Service Designer + UX Lead |
| **G4 — Pilot** | KPIs met, no critical operational failures, staff ready | Service Lead + Operations |
| **G5 — Scale** | Rollout plan approved, training complete, feedback loop active | Service Lead + Sponsor |

---

## Resilience & Recovery

- Rollback plan for pilot if critical service failure occurs
- Escalation path for operational incidents during pilot and scale
- Contingency for key staff or technology dependency failure
- Feedback triage process to distinguish signal from noise
- Sunset plan for legacy service being replaced

---

## International Validity

| Standard | Scope |
|---|---|
| IDEO / Double Diamond | Service design methodology |
| ITIL 4 | IT-enabled service management |
| ISO 9241-210:2019 | Human-centred design for interactive systems |
| GDS Service Standard (UK) | Government digital service design |
| WCAG 2.2 AA | Web accessibility for digital services |
| ISO 23592:2021 | Service excellence principles |
| NPS / CSAT / CES | Customer experience measurement |

---

## Lock Condition

**Locks when:** Service is live at scale, feedback loop active, KPIs met for 60 days, and operational ownership transferred to BAU.

**Reopens when:** NPS/CSAT drops below threshold, major user need change, regulatory requirement, or technology dependency end-of-life.
