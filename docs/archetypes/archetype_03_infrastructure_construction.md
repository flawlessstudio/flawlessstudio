# Archetype 03 — Infrastructure / Construction

> **Flawless Method** | `docs/archetypes/` | v1.0.0 | 2026-05-24

---

## Identity

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Output type** | Built physical asset — building, bridge, road, energy facility, civil infrastructure, or major structural installation |
| **Novelty class** | Mark n — construction process known; site, specification, and regulatory context variable |
| **Primary standard** | ISO 21500:2021, PRINCE2, RIBA Plan of Work 2020, Eurocodes, ISO 19650 (BIM) |
| **Sector** | Civil Engineering, Real Estate, Energy, Transport, Water, Public Infrastructure |

---

## Scope

All activities from site appraisal and feasibility through design, procurement, construction, commissioning, and handover of a built physical asset. Covers new build, renovation, fit-out, and infrastructure upgrade projects.

---

## Exclusions

| Out of scope | Redirect to |
|---|---|
| Manufacturing plant setup (process equipment) | Archetype 06 |
| IT infrastructure deployment inside a building | Archetype 10 |
| Engineering systems integration without construction | Archetype 04 |
| Environmental remediation as primary output | Archetype 20 |

---

## Primary Use Cases

- Delivering a commercial or residential building from planning to handover
- Constructing transport infrastructure (road, rail, bridge, port)
- Building an energy facility (solar farm, substation, data centre shell)
- Major refurbishment or fit-out of an existing structure
- Civil infrastructure upgrade (water treatment, drainage, utilities)

---

## Phase Structure

| # | Phase (RIBA aligned) | Key outputs |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | **Strategic definition** | Business case, feasibility, site appraisal, brief |
| 1 | **Preparation & briefing** | Project brief, site surveys, planning pre-application |
| 2 | **Concept design** | Concept design, structural and MEP strategy, cost plan L1 |
| 3 | **Spatial coordination** | Developed design, planning submission, cost plan L2 |
| 4 | **Technical design** | Production information, specialist design, tender package |
| 5 | **Construction** | Site works, quality inspections, progress reporting |
| 6 | **Handover** | Commissioning, O&M manuals, defects liability, practical completion |
| 7 | **Use** | Post-occupancy evaluation, lessons learned |

---

## Quality Controls

- BIM model (ISO 19650) maintained as single source of truth
- Independent cost review at each design stage gate
- Structural and MEP sign-off by chartered engineers before construction
- Site quality inspections on defined frequency (weekly minimum)
- Defects log maintained and closed before practical completion
- Health, safety and environmental (HSE) plan enforced throughout construction
- Third-party clerk of works or site inspector appointed for major works

---

## Validation Gates

| Gate | Criteria | Who approves |
|---|---|---|
| **G0 — Feasibility** | Business case approved, site viable, budget committed | Client + QS |
| **G1 — Planning** | Planning consent granted, design frozen at RIBA 3 | Architect + PM |
| **G2 — Procurement** | Contractor appointed, contract signed, programme agreed | PM + Commercial |
| **G3 — Construction start** | Site set up, HSE plan active, insurance in place | PM + HSE Lead |
| **G4 — Practical completion** | All works complete, inspections passed, O&M delivered | Client + Contract Admin |
| **G5 — Defects** | Defects liability period closed, final account agreed | PM + QS |

---

## Resilience & Recovery

- Contingency budget minimum 10% of construction cost
- Programme float built into critical path (minimum 4 weeks)
- Contractor insolvency protocol: step-in rights and performance bond
- Weather and ground risk mitigation in programme
- Force majeure protocol defined in contract
- Defects liability period minimum 12 months post-completion

| ISO 14001:2015 | Environmental management on site |
| FIDIC contracts | International construction contracts |
| NEC4 | Engineering and construction contract (international) |

---

## Lock Condition

**Locks when:** Practical completion certified, defects liability period closed, final account agreed, O&M manuals handed over, and post-occupancy evaluation published.

**Reopens when:** Major defect discovered, refurbishment required, change of use, or regulatory compliance breach identified.
